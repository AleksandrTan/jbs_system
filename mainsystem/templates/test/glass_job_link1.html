St. Patrick’s Manor is a nonprofit, faith-based, short-term and long-term care facility sponsored by the Carmelite Sisters for over 50 years. St. Patrick’s Manor is a place of life, a place of caring, and more importantly, a true home its residents. Located on lush, lawns and gardens in Framingham, MA, we are just minutes from Route 9 for easy access to the Mass Pike.

Carmel Terrace offers personalized assisted living and supportive services in a gracious residential setting.

We are looking for an experienced Director of Human Resources.

St. Patrick's Manor can offer you:

  • Competitive pay
  • Health, dental, vision, HSA with employer contribution
  • Flexible Spending Account
  • AFLAC supplemental insurances
  • Fully paid life and short-term disability insurance
  • Retirement plan
  • Employee Assistance Program
  • On-site training and skills enhancement
  • A mission-based approach to providing nursing care
  • Compassionate work environment

Director of Human Resources Job Summary:

  • Manages the recruiting and onboarding function through use of Indeed and the UKG (formerly Ultimate Software) recruiting/onboarding tool and coordination with hiring managers for candidate communication, interviews, hiring, and onboarding.
  • Ensures new personnel receive pre-employment criminal background check, physical, TB testing, and orientation.
  • Oversees management of time clock administration and its integration with processing payroll.
  • Oversees the payroll liaison (HR Coordinator) function and operates the UKG payroll and HRIS system.
  • Administrates the Payroll-Based Journal (PBJ) function for quarterly CMS nursing home hours worked regulation.
  • Is knowledgeable of all SPM/CT-specific shift and job duty differentials and the paid-time-off structure as it relates to payroll.
  • Is well-informed and effectively communicates information on employee benefits programs to include payroll deductions, PTO, health, dental and vision insurance, COBRA, life, supplemental insurances, and retirement plan.
  • Manages SPM/CT-specific Short-Term Disability claims in coordination with FMLA and MA Paid Family Leave.
  • Manages employee relations issues including employee disciplinary and termination actions, legal actions and consults with legal counsel as appropriate.
  • Creates and implements employee morale programs.
  • Manages all employee termination procedures and documents, including exit interviews, COBRA, unemployment insurance and employment verifications.
  • Manages the periodic employee evaluation process.
  • Maintains Employee Handbook, job descriptions, performance evaluations, and all other HR-related documents.
  • Maintains personnel files in accordance with established policies and procedures to include all required state and federal forms and records of required training.
  • Maintains current and accurate EEO-1 reports, PBJ and OSHA injury logs and complying with OSHA regulations.
  • Maintains responsibility for compliance with federal, state (including Massachusetts and surrounding states as necessary) and local labor law and regulation.
  • Supervises the staff of the Human Resources Department.

This is a brief overview of job responsibilities and not intended to be all inclusive.

Director of Human Resources Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s Degree is required and 7 to 10 years of HR experience or equivalent combination of education and experience.
  • Must be proficient in use of MS Office programs such as Excel, Word, and Outlook.
  • Strong interpersonal conflict resolution, employee relations and coaching skills.
  • Excellent organizational skill and customer service.
  • Experience with UKG software and/or On-Shift desired.
  • Advanced degree and SHRM-CP/SCP or HRCI-PHR/SPHR strongly preferred.
